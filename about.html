My Story

I Have Sat in Every Chair
in This Industry.

I started in this industry over 35 years ago and I have not left. I have owned and operated a high-quality collision repair shop for 15 years. I have written thousands of estimates. I have negotiated with adjusters, fought supplements, and watched good shops get squeezed by carriers who set rates based on what they want to pay — not what it actually costs to repair a vehicle correctly.

I am also a licensed Texas insurance adjuster. That is not a conflict — it is an advantage. I understand how carriers think, how adjusters are trained to push back, and exactly what documentation makes a denial impossible to sustain. I use that knowledge entirely on behalf of the shop.

I am not a software company. I am not an estimating platform with insurer partnerships. I am not a consultant who learned the industry from a classroom. I am someone who has lived every aspect of running a shop — the payroll, the equipment costs, the fights with adjusters, the responsibility to every vehicle owner who trusts you with their safety.

Aequus Claims exists because I believe shops deserve to be paid fairly for work performed correctly — and because I know exactly how to make that happen.

Why Aequus?

Aequus is Latin for Fair and Just.

The name was chosen deliberately. In an industry where insurance companies have systematically suppressed labor rates for decades while posting record profits, fairness is not a given — it is something you have to fight for. Every estimate Aequus Claims writes is a step toward a more equitable industry for shops, technicians, and the customers who trust them with their vehicles and their safety.

Safe Repair Advocacy

Safe Repair
Saves Lives.

This is not just a slogan. Modern vehicle structures are engineered to specific material grades, weld specifications, and dimensional tolerances. A repair performed without following OEM procedures does not just affect the appearance of a vehicle — it compromises the structural integrity that protects occupants in the next collision.

Every estimate Aequus writes documents the correct procedures — not just to get paid, but because those procedures exist for a reason. A properly documented estimate is also a properly repaired vehicle.

When accuracy is not optional, neither is full payment for doing it right.
