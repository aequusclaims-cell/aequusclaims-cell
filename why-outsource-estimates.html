Why Outsource Estimates?

May 28, 2026  ·  Oscar A. Hoyt IV, Aequus Claims
Oscar A. Hoyt IV
Founder, Aequus Claims  ·  I-CAR Gold Appraiser  ·  TX Licensed Adjuster  ·  35+ Years Industry

I started in the collision industry in the early 1990s. To be honest, my motive as a lost and confused teen was that it was a three-hour class I could skip in high school and it'd only count as one absence. Well, 35+ years later, the joke's on me. I'm still in love with this industry and the challenges it keeps bringing — as much as I was back when I painted my first car in that high school shop class. A 1973 VW Thing with Imron orange enamel. Short sleeves on. First through third period. IYKYK.

When I started as an apprentice, there were no computers in shops yet. We had those large, thick Mitchell books with simplistic line drawings on paper so thin it made Bible pages look like posterboard. If you wanted current prices, you called the dealer for quotes. While procedures were available, they were difficult to get. Most repair decisions were made under the premise of "I'd put my daughter in that repair." I always wondered — why just daughters?

How the Landscape Changed

Then, in the course of a few years, a few things happened that reshaped the collision landscape. Computers made estimating easier and more consistent. The internet allowed shops to find procedures cheaper. Social media made it easy for shops to compare notes. And the very public Seebachan v. John Eagle Collision Center lawsuit verdict woke some shops up to the importance of following those "recommended" procedures — and that their estimate and repair can come back to haunt them, or cost them $31.5 million.

One would hope that after seeing a family's quality of life destroyed and a shop hung out to dry because they listened to a material vendor instead of the manufacturer, shops across the country would have done an about-face. Despite all the change and technology to help shops write accurate repair costs and make correct decisions, it's still difficult to write an accurate estimate. Why? Because vehicles have increased in complexity and the demands on estimators have never been higher.

The Estimator Is Your Most Valuable and Most Burdened Employee

As sure as you are reading this, you know that as soon as you sit down to write that quarter panel, your phone is going to ring. That insurance adjuster is going to pop in without an appointment. A walk-in with a rolling total will demand an estimate. That tech is going to come tell you that you have a wrong part. Or a million other things you're tasked with on top of writing what is essentially an engineering research project.

Estimators are the salesmen, customer service reps, head negotiators, fire putter-outers, therapists, and parts people. They are by far one of the most important and vital parts of a shop. The shop's profitability and liability are on their shoulders, and they rarely get enough credit for it. Maybe if they all wore capes like most superheroes, they'd be seen for what they are.

What If There Was an Easy Button?

What if there was a solution that could help your estimator close more sales, be a better negotiator, and deliver customer service that turns Chick-fil-A's head? What if your estimator could push a button and a complex, well-documented repair cost was delivered to their computer — along with a supporting folder full of procedures and docs, nicely noted for easy explanation?

That solution exists: outsource your estimating to specialists who live and breathe this stuff.

In my 35 years, including owning an OE-certified shop for a decade, I've seen in-house estimators burn out from juggling too much. Simple mistakes were made. Rental limits exceeded because it took too long to get the repair quote correct. Outsourcing frees your estimator to focus on what they do best — selling repairs, building customer relationships, and negotiating with insurers. You get a complete estimate delivered fast, backed by OE procedures, best practices, photos, and references that prove every line item's necessity.

The Numbers Back It Up

Industry data shows 63% of collision repairs need at least one supplement, per CCC Intelligent Solutions' 2024 report. Poorly documented estimates are a significant reason for pushback. With outsourced estimates, shops see higher approval rates — I've helped clients hit 70–80% success on supplements because the documentation provides real leverage in disputes, appraisals, or even court cases.

The cost? Often less than maintaining a full-time in-house estimator when you factor in avoided rework — which can eat 15–20% of profits — and faster throughput.

Take a real example: A mid-sized Texas shop was losing $1,200–$1,800 per RO on denied supplements due to incomplete docs. They started outsourcing their larger claims and insurers approved 85% upfront — no back-and-forth. They saved hours per estimate, retained more customers, and reduced liability risks.
